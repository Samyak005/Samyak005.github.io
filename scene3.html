Deaths per million v/s GDP per capita

  • The US has the highest number of deaths per million when compared to countries with similar GDP per capita. Qatar and Singapore have the lowest deaths per million for the cohort with more than US$70000 per person as GDP per capita.
  • The UK, France, Sweden, Belgium, and Austria have the highest deaths per million for GDP per capita between US$40,000 to US$70,000. Kuwait and Australia have the lowest deaths per million in this set.
  • Slovakia, Greece, Latvia, Lithuania, Slovenia, and Italy have the highest deaths per capita and Saudi Arabia, Japan, and Brunei have the lowest deaths per capita for countries having GDP per capita between US$20,000 to US$40,000.
  • Croatia, Romania, and Chile have the highest number of deaths per million, and Kazakhstan, Maldives, and Dominion Republic have the lowest deaths per million for the countries having GDP per capita between US$10,000 to US$20,000.
  • China has been excluded from the analysis because of multiple reports indicating that the data is not accurate.
  • Peru, Bosnia, North Macedonia, Georgia, and Brazil have the highest deaths per million for countries with GDP per capita less than US$10,000. India, Vietnam, Egypt, Algeria, and Indonesia, among the large countries, were on the lower side in terms of deaths per million.
