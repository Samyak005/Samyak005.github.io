Deaths per million v/s GDP of country

  • One of the fears at the beginning of the pandemic was that developed countries (with higher GDP per capita) will dominate the vaccination program and will be able to manage the pandemic better than the less developed countries.
  • When we look at deaths per million relative to the GDP of the countries, we can notice 2 clear outliers US and Peru which demonstrate much higher death rates among their GDP peer set.
  • Among the next 20 large economies, western nations like Germany, Italy, France, Spain, Italy, and countries like Brazil, Mexico and Russia had much higher deaths per million.
  • Among the G20 countries, India, Indonesia, Saudi Arabia, Australia, South Korea, Canada had much lower deaths per million
  • When we look at next set of 50 countries by GDP, the distinction between Europe and South America and other countries is again clearly visible. European and South American countries show a much higher deaths per million within their peer set.
  • Even among the smaller countries by GDP, Asia and Africa demonstrate lower death rates per million
